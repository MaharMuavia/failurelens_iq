## Diagnosis
The evaluation protocol used accuracy as the primary headline metric on a severely imbalanced 90/10 churn dataset. A simple non-stratified train-test split allowed the XGBoost model to achieve 93% accuracy by correctly classifying the vast majority class while almost completely failing to identify churners, driving minority F1 down from a baseline of 0.65 to 0.14. No class-weighting, stratified sampling, or minority-focused threshold tuning was applied, violating standard imbalanced-classification practice.
- Violated assumption: That accuracy would be a reliable proxy for model quality when the operationally critical positive class (churn) constitutes only 10% of the data.
- Knowledge gap: The team did not internalize DP-100 guidance that accuracy is misleading on skewed data and that minority F1, recall, precision, confusion-matrix shape, and stratified folds must be the primary evaluation instruments.
- Evidence strength: strong

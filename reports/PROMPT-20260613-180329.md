## Diagnosis
The evaluation methodology relied on accuracy (0.93) as the headline metric for a 90/10 imbalanced churn prediction task. In such settings a model can achieve high accuracy simply by predicting the majority class on nearly all examples, producing a catastrophic drop in minority-class performance (F1 fell from baseline 0.65 to 0.14). No class-weighted loss, resampling, or appropriate minority-focused metrics (F1, recall, PR-AUC, confusion-matrix shape) were applied, exactly matching the DP-100 guidance that accuracy alone is misleading for rare operational classes.
- Violated assumption: Accuracy is a sufficient and representative performance measure for binary classification regardless of class distribution.
- Knowledge gap: Team lacks training on imbalanced-classification evaluation (minority F1/recall, threshold selection, cost-sensitive metrics) and on configuring XGBoost for imbalance (scale_pos_weight, eval_metric='aucpr').
- Evidence strength: strong

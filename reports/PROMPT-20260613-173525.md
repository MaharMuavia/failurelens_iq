## Diagnosis
The model evaluation relied primarily on accuracy (0.93) as the headline metric on a severely imbalanced 90/10 churn dataset. In such distributions a trivial majority-class classifier can achieve ~90% accuracy while completely failing to identify churners. The minority F1 collapsing from a baseline of 0.65 to 0.14 confirms the model is not separating the rare positive class; the evaluation methodology never surfaced this operational failure because it did not mandate threshold-aware or imbalance-robust metrics (minority F1, PR-AUC, confusion-matrix shape).
- Violated assumption: Accuracy is a reliable and sufficient performance metric for churn-prediction models.
- Knowledge gap: Team lacks training on imbalanced-classification evaluation (DP-100 guidance): when the positive class is 10% or less, accuracy must be de-emphasized in favor of minority-class recall, F1, precision, false-negative cost, and threshold optimization.
- Evidence strength: strong

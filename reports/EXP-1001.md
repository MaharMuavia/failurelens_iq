## Diagnosis
The evaluation pipeline used accuracy as the headline metric on an 88/12 imbalanced dataset together with a non-stratified holdout validation split. This combination allowed a model that performed poorly on the minority class (minority_f1 dropped from baseline 0.42 to 0.14) to report a deceptively high accuracy of 0.93, masking the regression relative to the previous baseline (accuracy 0.89, roc_auc 0.77). The holdout report did not surface fold-level variance or minority-class confusion-matrix behavior, violating standard practice for imbalanced classification.
- Violated assumption: Accuracy on a single holdout split is a reliable and sufficient indicator of model quality for imbalanced (88/12) classification problems.
- Knowledge gap: Team lacked practical application of DP-100 guidance on imbalanced metrics (minority F1, precision, false-negative cost, confusion-matrix shape) and the necessity of stratified k-fold validation (or explicit stratification of holdouts) when data is IID. The taxonomy highlights that accuracy alone hides minority-class failures on skewed distributions.
- Evidence strength: strong
